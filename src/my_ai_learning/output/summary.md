- AI is now a billion‑dollar industry, worth about $1.9 trillion in 2023 and expected to grow to $8.5 trillion by 2030, with companies spending heavily on AI cloud services and hardware.  
- The breakthrough of **foundation models**—huge, multimodal systems like GPT‑4, Gemini 1, and Claude 3—has enabled machines to understand and generate text, images, audio, and video all in one model, driving rapid adoption across many sectors.  
- AI is already reshaping key fields: faster medical diagnoses and drug design, smarter financial fraud detection, automated legal contract review, personalized education, creative content generation, and more efficient manufacturing and public‑service operations.  
- Major concerns include bias, misinformation, privacy violations, IP uncertainty, safety/alignment risks, and the high energy cost of training massive models, prompting industry‑wide calls for better auditing, watermarking, and greener training methods.  
- Governments worldwide are acting: the EU’s AI Act, the U.S. AI Executive Order, China’s regulation on deep‑generation AI, and similar laws cover about two‑thirds of global GDP, forcing companies to adopt risk‑by‑design and compliance frameworks.
